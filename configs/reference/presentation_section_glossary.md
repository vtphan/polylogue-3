# Presentation Section Glossary

Definitions for the five presentation sections. Referenced by section-generator and evaluator.

---

| Section | Purpose | Expected content | Common flaw locations |
|---------|---------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Introduction** | Frame the problem and the team's angle | Driving question, why it matters, preview of what the team did | Framing too narrow/broad; unstated assumptions; vague problem definition |
| **Approach** | Explain what the team did or proposes | Research process, design choices, methods, rationale for the approach | Gaps in methodology; unjustified choices; approach disconnected from the problem |
| **Findings** | Present supporting material | Data, research results, examples, prototype demonstration | Overstated evidence; selective data; correlation as causation; vague claims |
| **Solution** | State what the team recommends or built | The product, plan, or design — the team's answer to the driving question | Missing feasibility; unaddressed tradeoffs; no stakeholder analysis; ignoring constraints |
| **Conclusion** | Wrap up and reflect | Summary, limitations, next steps, connection back to driving question | Claims beyond what evidence supports; overgeneralization; ignoring limitations |

Not every presentation requires all five sections. Simpler projects may combine Approach and Findings. The sections are a default structure.

The **Approach** and **Conclusion** sections are natural homes for a synthesizer or connector agent, since both require pulling together other agents' contributions. Assigning one agent to both amplifies coherence flaw opportunities — the same agent's knowledge gaps shape how the presentation opens and closes.

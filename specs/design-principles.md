# Design Principles

Core principles governing Polylogue 3's architecture.

---

## 1. Loose on Content, Tight on Structure

Polylogue 3 delegates creative interpretation to LLMs while enforcing rigorous structure on inputs, outputs, and pipeline communication.

**Loose on content** means: agent personas are described in natural language, not numerical dimensions. Knowledge profiles use qualitative categories (strong understanding, shallow understanding, misconception, blind spot) rather than scores. Dispositions are three broad dimensions plus a qualitative reactive tendency, not twelve scored dimensions. The LLM interprets these rich descriptions to generate natural, varied behavior.

**Tight on structure** means: every pipeline stage has defined input and output schemas. Outputs use enumerated values where consistency matters. Required fields are enforced. Registry state is structured YAML. Metadata is preserved for downstream stages even when hidden from agents.

**Why:** Polylogue 3 is more general than Polylogue 2 but simpler in its agent specifications. This works because LLMs are capable of interpreting rich qualitative descriptions into coherent behavior. But LLM creativity must operate within a reliable pipeline — otherwise multi-round generation drifts, formats become inconsistent, and downstream stages (evaluation, analysis) break.

## 2. LLM Generation with Human Curation

Scenarios, profiles, and transcripts are generated by LLMs. Humans curate and revise — they don't author from scratch.

**Implications:**
- Specifications must be **readable and editable** by humans who are not system designers. A teacher should be able to revise a knowledge profile without understanding the full architecture.
- The workflow has **explicit human checkpoints**: after scenario generation, after profile generation. No discourse is generated until the human approves the setup.
- Expected flaw annotations exist in profiles for human curators but are **excluded from personas** to avoid biasing LLM generation.

## 3. Knowledge-Primary Flaw Generation

Knowledge profiles are the primary source of critical thinking flaws. Dispositions shape how flaws are expressed and drive interaction dynamics, but the *what* of the flaw comes from knowledge gaps.

**Implication:** The design workflow is topic-first. Scenarios define expected flaws; profiles are designed backward from those flaws; knowledge gaps are the mechanism.

## 4. Metadata Splitting

Agents (personas) see only what a real participant would see: conversation content and their own character. System metadata — expected flaws, flaw annotations, stage tracking signals — is hidden from agents but preserved for orchestration and evaluation.

**Why:** Exposing system metadata to agents biases generation. An agent that knows it's "supposed to" exhibit a completeness flaw will overperform that flaw. An agent that sees intensity ratings from other agents will respond to the rating, not to the content.

## 5. Emergent Behavior

Flaws emerge from agent characteristics and context — they are not scripted. Expected flaws are predictions, not instructions. The architecture creates conditions for flaws to surface naturally.

**Carries over from Polylogue 2.** This principle is unchanged.
